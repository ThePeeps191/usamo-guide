## Overview

Generating functions encode sequences into algebraic objects. Even simple
applications can simplify counting and recurrence problems.

## Key Ideas

- The coefficient of $x^n$ in $(1+x+x^2+\cdots)^k$ counts solutions to
  $x_1+\cdots+x_k=n$ with nonnegative integers.
- Finite options become finite polynomials like $(1+x+x^2)$. 
- Multiplying series corresponds to combining choices.

## Worked Example

Count solutions to $a+b+c=5$ with $a,b,c\ge0$.

The generating function is $(1+x+x^2+\cdots)^3=\frac{1}{(1-x)^3}$. The
coefficient of $x^5$ is $\binom{5+3-1}{3-1}=21$.
