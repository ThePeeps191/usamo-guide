## Overview

Euclidean geometry problems rely on angle chasing and similarity. Power of a
point and cyclic quadrilaterals are frequent tools in AMC 12 and AIME.

## Key Ideas

- If a quadrilateral is cyclic, opposite angles sum to $180^\circ$.
- Power of a point: for chords through a point, $PA\cdot PB=PC\cdot PD$.
- Similar triangles yield proportional sides and equal angles.

## Worked Example

In a circle, chords $AB$ and $CD$ intersect at $P$. If $PA=3$, $PB=5$, and
$PC=2$, then $PD=\frac{PA\cdot PB}{PC}=\frac{15}{2}$.
